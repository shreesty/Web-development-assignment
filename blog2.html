Social Media Bringing You Down? 3 Ways to Protect Your Mental Health

Social media has become an integral part of our lives, but it can also have a negative impact on our mental health. Here are four ways to protect your mental health while still enjoying social media:

Filter Your Feed

One of the biggest sources of negativity on social media is the content we consume. It's important to filter your feed and remove any content that makes you feel uncomfortable or unhappy. You can use CSS to create filters that block certain keywords, hashtags, or even entire accounts from appearing on your feed.

Set Limits on Usage

Another way to protect your mental health is by setting limits on your social media usage. This can be as simple as limiting the amount of time you spend scrolling each day or as strict as scheduling specific times for social media use. HTML can be used to create reminders or notifications that help you stick to your limits and avoid overuse.

Connect with Positive People

Social media is a great way to connect with people, but not all connections are equal. Surrounding yourself with positive people who uplift and support you is important for your mental health. You can use CSS to highlight positive posts or profiles and de-emphasize negative ones, making it easier to find and connect with people.
